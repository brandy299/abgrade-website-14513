About the Network

Who We Are

An independent organisation built by early-career researchers, for early-career researchers — connecting the next generation of astrobiologists across Europe.

Our Mission

What is AbGradE?

AbGradE (Astrobiology Graduates in Europe) is an independent organisation with the goal of inspiring, educating and promoting networking amongst early-career scientists and students in astrobiology, space science and other related fields.

Our goals are achieved through events and activities — workshops, symposia, and keynote lectures — that promote astrobiology and space science in a stress-free, collaborative environment.

Each year, AbGradE hosts its signature event immediately before the EANA Astrobiology Meeting, following the theme of EANA's annual scientific focus topic. As a result, AbGradE has developed as a hub and incubator for new scientists to become involved in space-related research and to build academic connections.

AbGradE has formed strong collaborations with universities, scientific societies, and other entities — most notably with EANA (European Astrobiology Network Association), from which it originally stemmed, and recently with the EAI (European Astrobiology Institute).

Our Story

How It All Started

The original committee was formed over a few drinks at the conference dinner of the 13th EANA meeting in July 2013 in Szczecin, Poland. What began as an informal gathering of enthusiastic early-career researchers has since grown into a thriving European network — strongly supported by EANA from the very beginning.

Our members come from diverse backgrounds spanning the many disciplines that comprise astrobiology, space science, and beyond. All members can participate in every meeting and working group. The network continues to grow, welcoming researchers from across Europe and the world.
